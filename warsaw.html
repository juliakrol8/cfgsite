Warsaw

Warsaw is the capital and the largest city in Poland. It is its economic, political and cultural centre. It lies on the Vistula River and its symbol is the Mermaid.

It is the world's largest financial centre and is often regarded as a world cultural capital. It is the world's most-visited city as measured by international arrivals and has the world's largest city airport system measured by passenger traffic.

London has a diverse range of people and cultures, and more than 300 languages are spoken in the region. Its estimated mid-2016 municipal population, corresponding to Greater London, was 8,787,892, the largest of any city in the European Union and accounting for 13.4% of the UK population.

Food

  • Top Polish dishes
    1. Pierogi

      2. They are one of the most simple and most delicious dishes in Polish cuisine. Pierogi are dumplings made with sweet or savoury fillings. The most popular pierogi are with quark cheese, potatoes and onions (called ruskie), with meat (miesne), with sauerkraut and mushrooms (z kapusta i grzybami) or with blueberries (z jagodami). These are usually served with sour cream or fried onions.

    2. Bigos

      3. Bigos is a cabbage dish that is also filled with all kinds of meat like sausage or bacon. It is best enjoyed with a shot (or two) of vodka!

    3. Paczki

      4. Paczki are the Polish version of doughnuts. The doughnuts can be filled with many different types of flavours, however the most common filling is the rose jam. Paczki are traditionally eaten during Fat Thursday, which is the last Thursday before Lent. However, who would refuse to eat paczki all year round?

    4. Traditional soups

      5. There are many soups you need to try during your visit to Poland! Rosol is a chicken soup with noodles that most, if not all, Polish families eat on a Sunday. Barszcz is a beetroot soup that is usually served on Christmas Eve. It is accompanied by uszka (which literally translates to 'little ears'), which are little dumplings filled with meat or sauerkraut. On the other hand, zurek is a soup served during Easter made of soured rye flour. It is obligatory to serve it with a hard boiled egg!

    5. Golabki

      6. Golabki are another cabbage dish - a Polish version of cabbage rolls. To make them you need soft-boiled cabbage leaves and some minced pork or beef. There is also rice or barley, chopped onions and herbs. The rolls are baked in the oven in a casserole dish and served with a creamy tomato sauce.

    6. Nalesniki

      7. Nalesniki are (better) Polish version of crepes! They are usually served with sweet quark cheese, but can also be filled with jam, blueberries or apples.

  • Quirky restaurants in London
    1. Dinner by Heston Blumenthal
      2. Check official website

      Settle down for a quirky culinary experience at Dinner by Heston Blumenthal, a two Michelin-star establishment that's consistently voted one of the best restaurants in the world. The celebrity chef taps into Britain's rich culinary history with unusual results, such as the signature Meat Fruit starter, where chicken parfait is served in the guise of a clementine.

    2. Trader Vic's
      3. Check official website

      Descend into a tropical getaway in Mayfair as you escape into a world of South Sea dining, at Trader Vic's. Start with traditional finger food, pick up a salad or a soup, and for main opt for a Trader Vic favourite such as Beef & Reef or Scottish lobster.

    3. Bubba Gump Shrimp Co.
      4. Check official website

      Based on hit film Forrest Gump, this Southern American eatery is themed on its main characters and its Alabama location. It’s all about the shrimp at Bubba Gump Shrimp Co., with lots of crustacean creations; but there’s plenty for all the family to enjoy, from grilled steaks and salads to jambalaya.

    4. Bob Bob Ricard
      5. Check official website

      Soak up the glamorous atmosphere at this Soho dining hotspot. The Bob Bob Ricard menu features an array of British-Russian dishes to enjoy in private booths, surrounded by glitzy art-deco decor. Each table has a “press for champagne” button, meaning you can really dine in style during your trip to London.

    5. Beach Blanket Babylon
      6. Check official website

      Dining at Beach Blanket Babylon is like stepping between a Georgian English manor house and a French chateau. If the vintage furnishings, imposing statues and all-round extraordinary decor don't inspire awe at this celebrity hotspot, the seasonal, modern European food will.

    6. Inamo
      7. Check official website

      This Asian fusion restaurant in Covent Garden (with sister eateries in Soho and Camden) boasts a pioneering interactive ordering system projected onto the table, making it one of the coolest restaurants in London. At Inamo, you can place orders via a 3D menu, choose from a selection of virtual tablecloths, find out about the local neighbourhood and even order a taxi home, all from the comfort of your seat.
